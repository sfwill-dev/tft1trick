<a id="gameplan"></a>
### Gameplan

Throughout the game, look for these win conditions from augments, god offers, and god boons to increase your chances of hitting top 4 or even placing 1st:
**Special items** (Radiant, Artifacts, Anomaly [Ekko's Boon], Anima), **Special combat bonuses** (Solo Plate, BIS Yasuo hexes), **Econ** (to roll down on stage 4 and hit the board), **BIS items**.

_More details on the [itemization](#items) and [augments](#augments) sections._

<a id="stage2"></a>
### Stage 1-2

- Ideally play around an econ engine trait such as Primordian or Timebreaker. 
  - Anima can also be played, but it is high risk high reward and often requires very good skills, especially APM.
- Play the strongest board and save as much HP as possible, by slamming items, but only ones that are [good quality for Aurelion Sol or Mighty Mech](#items).
- If board is NOT strong and cannot win streak:
  - Level to 4 on stage 2-3.
  - Level to 5 on stage 2-7.
- If board is strong and can win streak:
  - Level to 4 on stage 2-1.
  - Level to 5 on stage 2-5.

<a id="stage3"></a>
### Stage 3

- Continue playing the strongest board, slamming the items you can, and saving as much HP as possible.
- If board is NOT strong and cannot win streak:
  - Level to 6 on stage 3-2.
  - Level to 7 when possible while keeping gold above 50
- If board is strong and can win streak:
  - Level to 6 on stage 3-1 if confident enough to keep the streak, otherwise level on 3-2.
  - Level to 7 on stage 3-5.

<a id="stage4"></a>
### Stage 4

- Decide when to level to 8:
  - ideally on stage 4-1 while keeping gold above 50, especially if there is no win condition so far, such as [special items](#items) or [special combat bonuses](#augments). 
  - otherwise, level on stage 4-2.
- Sell all units that won't be in the final board, then roll down for 2-star Mighty Mech > 2-star Asol > 2-star Urgot. 
  - Transform all mecha units and play 6 Mecha.
  - Buy and play just a single copy of Karma, Akali, and Maokai, if you haven't already.
    - When high rolling a Fiora, play it instead of Akali, especially if winning fights, since Fiora increases tactician health.
      - Then ideally play TK over Maokai.
  - If 2-star mecha units are still missing, roll to zero if necessary, and roll down again on stage 4-5. 
  Every turn you don't have them upgraded, the higher the chances to go bot 4.
  - Only after hitting the 2-star mecha units:
    - Econ up to level 9 ideally on stage 5, upgrading the rest of the board along the way.

<a id="stage5"></a>
### Stage 5+

- Level to 9 ideally on stage 5, but only after hitting the 2-star mecha units.
- Add a legendary unit. Jhin > Bard.
  - Mordekaiser can be played while not finding one of them, at least he gives two traits to the board (Dark Star + Conduit)
- When hitting a Fiora, decide if it is still worth adding her at this point (+ TK over Maokai):
  - It's good if winning fights, since Fiora increases tactician health.
  - If hitting 2-star Fiora + 2-star TK, it should be worth playing them over Akali + Maokai.
    - But holding them on bench in the meantime might not be worth it, e.g. if Karma or Jhin have good items, and there are no items for Fiora, rolling down to 2-star them might be more worth it, just keeping the N.O.V.A. units.
- Making 2-star Fiora or Jhin a secondary carry with good items can boost your placement.
- Level to 10 once everything is upgraded and add Bard. While it provides Conduit, that trait isn't as important for Asol.

### Tips
- Asol in Mecha form does not auto attack, making items like Shojin and Nashor's worthless.
  - And his [best itemization](#items) is pure damage. Mana items are not ideal or necessary.

<a id="when-contested"></a>
### How to play when contested
There is not much to do about being contested when playing this comp. 
You just need to hit 2-star Mighty Mech and 2-star Asol, otherwise you are likely bot 4, that is why we prioritize econ so much and roll down aggressively to hit them, caring almost nothing about the other units in the meantime.

A good thing is, currently Asol is usually not played outside of this comp, and Urgot is a 3-cost so there are more copies available. 
Mighty Mech is the one that can be more of a contested problem if many boards are playing it as a secondary tank and for the voyager trait.

That said, if you've scaled resistances on a Brawler through augments or hexes and don't hit 2-star Mighty Mech, you can play that Brawler with Mecha items in the meantime. 
In this specific scenario, if Urgot has no leftover items, consider not transforming Mighty Mech and Urgot, playing 4 Mecha instead, and adding a unit at level 8 like Mordekaiser, Jhin, or Bard, which you'd normally add at level 9.

<a id="items"></a>
### Items

- **Mighty Mech**: **2x Gargoyle's**, Sterak's
  - **Soraka's Miracle** (Divine Artifact) over Sterak's
  - **Artifacts** providing health over Sterak's
  - **Artifacts** providing resistances over second Gargoyle's
  - **Yasuo Hexes**, such as **Cryogenic** or Cosmic, are strong and can act as a 4th item
- **Aurelion Sol**: **Jeweled Gauntlet**, Deathcap, Striker's
  - Archangel's, Void Staff
  - **Divine Artifacts** such as Ahri's Aura, Ekko's Patience
  - **Artifacts** providing AP or damage amp
  - **Anomaly (Ekko's Boon)**
  - **Yasuo Hexes**, such as **Storm** or Solar, are strong and can act as a 4th item
- **Karma**: Red Buff/Morello, Void Staff, Gunblade
  - leftover AP
- Additional:
    - **Urgot**: TG
      - Left over AD, ideally transitioned later to Fiora or Jhin
    - **Jhin**: Infinity Edge, Last Whisper, Red Buff
      - Striker's, leftover AD
    - **Fiora**: Edge of Night, Hand of Justice, Sterak's
      - Bloodthirster, Titan's, QSS, leftover AD
    - **Tahm Kench**: Leftover tank
