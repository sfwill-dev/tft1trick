<h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold tracking-tight text-zinc-50">Common Questions:</h2>

**Q.: What is one-tricking?** <br/>
**A.:** A playstyle where the player forces the same comp (a group of units with synergies) every game, or nearly every game. It frees up mental energy during the game, since instead of trying to read their spot and picking a comp, they can focus on other fundamentals. It's beginner-friendly, and can still reportedly carry to any rank if the fundamentals keep pace.

**Q.: Does one-tricking mean I don't have to play flexibly?** <br/>
**A.:** Mostly no. It's true that the final board will look similar game to game when one-tricking, 4 to 8 core units plus a few situational additions (often legendaries). But early-mid game, especially at higher ranks, flexibility matters: slam items on whichever strong unit shows up, preserve HP, and transition into the final board when hit.

**Q.: Does one-tricking replace learning fundamentals?** <br/>
**A.:** No. It gives the player an _indirect_ edge by concentrating their focus, but no _direct_ advantage over opponents at the same rank. Fundamentals such as economy, itemization, and leveling still shape how high one can climb.

**Q.: Why isn't every comp forceable?** <br/>
**A.:** A comp ideally needs to meet these conditions for me to consider one-tricking it:

- **Strong enough to climb**. Solid average placement in the current data, ideally at least 4.5. 
  - Counterintuitively, an average placement that's *too* strong (such as 4.1 or better) tends to be worse for one-tricking, as it usually has a high play rate and will be contested more often.
- **No hard requirements.** no specific augment, no level 9+ minimum, no BIS items, no 3-star units. These aren't consistently achievable every game.
- **Flexible enough to salvage placements when contested.** If two or more players are playing our units, we need viable alternative tanks or carries. Some comps fit together so tightly (stacked 3-piece traits, etc.) that swapping a single unit makes them much weaker, those are hard to one-trick.

The above is my default mental model, but of course exceptions exist per set and patch, for example:
- A comp that normally needs BIS items might be fine to force if it's strong out of lose-streak openers, where we get more control over my components. Or we can try to find good viable item alternatives.
- In patches where there are no truly Fast 8 options (my preference for one-tricking), a Fast 8 comp that needs to go 9 to be complete can be one-tricked.
- When reroll comps are just too stronger than everything else, we might need to pivot out of the one-trick strategy and flex between 2 or 3 reroll comps depending on what is not being contested.
