<h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold tracking-tight text-zinc-50">Common Questions:</h2>

**Q.: What is one-tricking?** <br/>
**A.:** A playstyle where you force the same comp (a group of units with synergies) every game, or nearly every game. It frees up mental energy during the game, since instead of trying to read your spot and picking a comp, you can focus on other fundamentals. It's beginner-friendly, and can still reportedly carry you to any rank if your fundamentals keep pace.

**Q.: Does one-tricking mean I don't have to play flexibly?** <br/>
**A.:** Mostly no. It's true that your final board will look similar game to game, 4 to 8 core units plus a few situational additions (often legendaries). But early-mid game, especially at higher ranks, flexibility matters: slam items on whichever strong unit shows up, preserve HP, and transition into your final board when you hit.

**Q.: Does one-tricking replace learning fundamentals?** <br/>
**A.:** No. It gives you an _indirect_ edge by concentrating your focus, but no _direct_ advantage over opponents at your rank. Fundamentals such as economy, itemization, and leveling still shape how high you climb.

**Q.: Why isn't every comp forceable?** <br/>
**A.:** A comp needs to meet a few conditions before I'll consider one-tricking it:

- **Strong enough to climb**. Solid average placement in the current data, ideally at least 4.5. 
  - Counterintuitively, an average placement that's *too* strong (such as 4.1 or better) tends to be worse for one-tricking, as it usually has a high play rate and you'll be contested more often.
- **No hard requirements.** No specific augment, no level 9+ minimum, no BIS items (or viable alternatives). These aren't consistently achievable every game.
- **Flexible enough to salvage placements when contested.** If two or more players are playing your units, you need viable alternative tanks or carries. Some comps fit together so tightly (stacked 3-piece traits, etc.) that swapping a single unit makes them much weaker, those are hard to one-trick.
- **A Fast 8 comp.**
  - **Not a reroll comp**. They usually need to be uncontested or demand sharp pivot reads, and missing either gets punishing at higher ranks. On Fast 8, contest hurts less: even with the smaller 4-cost pool, hitting three copies instead of nine still tends to be easier. You can also often stabilize on a 1-star itemized carry, and replacement 4/5-costs should be easier to find on level 8/9 than a solid replacement would be at lower levels when rerolling.
  - **Not a Fast 9 comp**. They require enough econ to hit level 9 alive, which isn't consistently doable.

Exceptions exist per set and patch. A Fast 8 comp that normally needs BIS items might be fine to force if it's strong out of lose-streak openers, where you get more control over your components. And in patches where Fast 9 is both strong and reachable, a Fast 9 comp can become one-trickable. But the above is my default mental model.
