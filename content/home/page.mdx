<h2 className="text-2xl font-semibold tracking-tight text-zinc-50">Common Questions:</h2>

**Q.: What is one-tricking?** <br/>
**A.:** A playstyle where the player forces the same comp (a group of units with synergies) every game, or nearly every game. It frees up mental energy during the game because, instead of reading their spot and choosing a comp, they can focus on other fundamentals. It's beginner-friendly, and can still reportedly carry to any rank if the fundamentals keep pace.

**Q.: Does one-tricking mean the player doesn't have to play flexibly?** <br/>
**A.:** Mostly no. It's true that the final board looks similar game to game when one-tricking: usually 4 to 8 core units plus a few situational additions (often legendaries). But in early and mid game, especially at higher ranks, flexibility still matters: slam items on whichever strong unit shows up, preserve HP, and transition into the final board when hit.

**Q.: Does one-tricking replace learning fundamentals?** <br/>
**A.:** No. It gives the player an _indirect_ edge by concentrating their focus, but no _direct_ advantage over opponents at the same rank. Fundamentals such as economy, itemization, and leveling still shape how high the player can climb.

**Q.: Why isn't every comp forceable?** <br/>
**A.:** To me, a comp ideally needs to meet these conditions to be considered one-trickable:

- **Strong enough to climb**. Solid average placement in the current data, ideally at least 4.5. 
  - Counterintuitively, an average placement that's *too* strong (such as 4.1 or better) is often worse for one-tricking, because it usually means a high play rate and being contested more often.
- **No hard requirements.** No specific augment, no level 9+ minimum, no BIS items, no 3-star units. These aren't consistently achievable every game.
- **Flexible enough to salvage placements when contested.** If two or more players are playing our units, we need viable alternative tanks or carries. Some comps fit together so tightly (stacked 3-piece traits, etc.) that swapping a single unit makes them much weaker; those are hard to one-trick.

The above is my default mental model, but exceptions can exist in each set and patch. For example:
- A comp that normally needs BIS items might still be forceable if it's strong out of lose-streak openers, where we get more control over our components. We can also try to find good item alternatives.
- In patches where there are no strong Fast 8 options, a somewhat Fast 9 comp that stabilizes well at level 8 might still be considered one-trickable.
- When reroll comps are much stronger than everything else, we may need to pivot away from strict one-tricking and flex between 2 or 3 reroll comps, depending on what is least contested.
