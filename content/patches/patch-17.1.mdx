### Core idea

N.O.V.A. is a great trait where each unit adds a bonus to the team, and if all 5 units are played, one can be selected to become stronger and grant an additional bonus to the team.

Since all units are low-cost (1-3 cost), except Kindred (4 cost), it seems to be designed as a splash trait where 1-4 units are played based on what fits the board to get their bonuses.

But each unit also has a good secondary trait that synergizes with 4-cost or 5-cost units, making 5 N.O.V.A. a strong flexible core for a one-trick comp in my opinion and from my experience playing it.

### Stage 1-2

- Level to 4 ideally on stage 2-1, then play the strongest opener while slamming Tank, AD Marksman, and Fighter items, according to the components received.
Save as much HP as possible. 
  - Don't slam AD caster or AP items.
  - Don't slam Sunder/Shred items, as both are provided by Aatrox.

### Stage 3

- Level to 6 ideally by stage 3-2.
- Continue playing the strongest board and slamming items.

### Stage 4

- Level to 8 ideally by stage 4-2, then roll down for 2-star TK, 2-star Kindred, and whatever 5 costs are found, such as 1-star Fiora. 
The core synergies are 5 N.O.V.A. + 2 Bastion + 2 Brawler + 2 Marauder.
  - When getting a N.O.V.A emblem, play the [Emblem version](#5nova-emblem) by dropping Caitlyn, not playing Graves, and playing 4 Marauder instead.
  - While Fiora is not found, Fighter item holders can be:
    - Master Yi > Akali > Bel'Veth
  - While Shen is not found, the Bastion unit can be:
    - Rammus > Ornn
  - While 2-star TK is not found, Tank item holders can be:
    - Rammus > Maokai = Ornn

### Stage 5+

- Once stable, level to 9 as soon as possible and look for the final board.
  - If there is not enough money to go level 9 and roll for multiple 2-star 5-cost units, play the [Budget version](#5nova-budget) and just add Sona at level 9 for Psionic synergy, or whatever 5-cost is found, such as Morgana.
    - It's not as strong for placing 1st or 2nd, but top-4 can still be achieved consistently in my experience so far, especially with an early/mid-game tempo.
  - If 2-star Kindred is too contested, 2-star Graves can hold Kindred items and be the main carry.
    - 2-star Vex can also hold Kindred items, except Kraken's.

### Items
- **Kindred**: **Guinsoo's**, Kraken's, Striker's
  - Giant Slayer, Deathblade, Titan's
- **Tahm Kench**: Sunfire Cape, Generic Tank 
  - Don't slam Sunder/Shred items, as both are provided by Aatrox
- **Fiora**: Hand of Justice, Edge of Night, Sterak's
  - Bloodthirster, Titan's, QSS, Generic Fighter
- **Graves**: Hand of Justice, QSS, Sterak's
  - Bloodthirster, Titan's, Generic Fighter 
- **Shen**: Leftover Fighter/Tank items

### Augments

- Augments should be selected based on what is needed at each stage of the game, e.g., whether the opener gives more champions or items, what the encounter is, and whether win-streaking or lose-streaking is being played.
- That said, I prefer an **early/mid-game tempo** playstyle with this comp, so in most games I lean toward augments that provide immediate power over those that scale later.
  - This usually means **Items**, but **some Econ options** can also be good, such as two Lesser Champion Duplicators to 2-star a 3-cost unit that fits the board and can be played until stage 4.
  - Also, since level 9 is the goal when possible, augments that give **both immediate power and econ** are strong, such as a Lucky Item Chest + some gold.
